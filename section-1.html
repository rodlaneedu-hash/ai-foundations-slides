What is AI?

Four key distinctions that change how you think about this technology.

The broad field

Artificial Intelligence

Any system designed to perform tasks that normally require human intelligence.

This is a field of computer science, not a single technology. When people say "AI" they could mean almost anything.

What exists today

Narrow AI

AI trained for a specific task. It does one thing well. It doesn't "think."

Spam filters Voice assistants Netflix suggestions Predictive text

General AI (AGI)

A hypothetical machine that can perform any intellectual task a human can.

This is the sci-fi version. Terminator. HAL 9000. Sentient robots.

It does not exist.
Nobody serious can tell you when — or if — it will.

This is what we're here for

Generative AI

A subset of narrow AI that creates new content — text, images, code, audio, video.

ChatGPT Claude Gemini Copilot Midjourney

Powerful and useful — but still narrow AI. This is what the rest of the journey is about.

It's not going to wake up and become conscious.

Generative AI does not understand and it cannot do everything. That distinction matters for every decision you'll make about how to use it.
