Phase 3: Engage

Three Ways to Use
Generative AI

The bridge from understanding to doing.
Once you've decided AI fits the task, choose the right kind of engagement.

1
Mode 1
Automation
"Do this for me."

You give the AI a clear, specific instruction. It completes the task. You check the result.

Summarise this document into five bullet points
Reformat this data into a table
Draft a follow-up email from meeting notes
Translate this into plain language

Automation: when it fits

Works best when

The task is routine, the instruction is specific, and you can check the output quickly. You're saving time on work you already know how to do.

Doesn't work when

The task is ambiguous, requires nuance, or you wouldn't recognise a wrong answer. Automation with no verification is just outsourcing your mistakes.

2
Mode 2
Augmentation
"Think with me."

You and the AI work together as thinking partners. You're not handing over a task — you're having a conversation.

What angles could I take for a Year 8 unit on climate change?
Here's my draft strategy — what am I missing?
Talk me through options for structuring this report
Challenge my assumptions about this approach

Augmentation: when it fits

Works best when

The task is complex, open-ended, or creative. You have the expertise to evaluate what comes back, and you're using the AI to extend your thinking rather than replace it.

Doesn't work when

You're looking for a definitive answer rather than a conversation. If you defer to whatever the AI suggests, you've slipped back into automation without the verification.

3
Mode 3
Agency
"Act on my behalf."

The AI works more independently, making decisions within boundaries you've set. You're the director, not the scriptwriter.

Monitor my inbox and categorise by urgency
Research five companies and produce a comparison brief
Build a first draft of this website from my spec

Agency: when it fits

Works best when

You have a clear brief, well-defined boundaries, and a review process before anything goes live. You trust the tool enough to give it room, but you still check before it reaches anyone else.

Doesn't work when

The boundaries are unclear, there's no review step, or the consequences of a bad decision are significant. It will proceed with full assurance regardless of whether it's getting things right.

Scriptwriter to Director

Chat

You type a prompt, read the response, and follow up. Back and forth, one message at a time. You control every step.

Where things are heading

You don't pick one mode. In a single session, you might automate a formatting task, augment your planning, then go back to automation. The skill is matching the mode to the task.

5
Key Landing Point
Automation is the entry point.
Augmentation is where the real value lives.
Agency is where things are heading —
but only with the right guardrails.

These three modes are a practical framework for deciding how to engage with AI once you've decided to use it.
