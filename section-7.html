Section 7

Core Skills for Working with AI

The 4Ds Framework

Four professional skills that make AI useful.
From Anthropic's AI Fluency training.

D1
D1

Delegation

Strategy — Deciding what to hand over
  • Know your goal before involving AI. Define success criteria and the type of work involved.
  • Know what AI is good at — speed, scale, first drafts, brainstorming. Not judgment, accuracy, or context.
  • Choose the right involvement — automate it, collaborate on it, reserve it for humans, or delegate to an agent.
D2
D2

Description

Communication — Telling it what you need
  • What to produce — the output. Goal, context, source material, expectations (tone, length, structure, format).
  • How to work — the process. Lay out the steps. Give a methodology, not just an end goal.
  • How to behave — challenge assumptions, be concise, explain reasoning, flag uncertainties.
D3
D3

Discernment

Evaluation — The most important skill
  • Evaluate the output — accurate? Relevant? Complete? Watch for the 95%-right answer with one buried error.
  • Evaluate the reasoning — logical path? Circular reasoning? Has it reintroduced a rejected idea?
  • Evaluate the interaction — too verbose? Too cautious? Too agreeable? Fix with better description.
D4
D4

Diligence

Responsibility — Your name is on it
  • Be intentional about which tools you use. Consider data handling, accuracy, and safety.
  • Be transparent about AI's role. If AI shaped the substance, say so.
  • Verify before you share — check for accuracy, bias, and appropriateness. Higher stakes means a more rigorous check.
The 4D Cycle
1 Delegate
2 Describe
3 Discern
4 Diligence

➜

A cycle, not a linear sequence. When discernment reveals a problem, loop back to better description — or revisit delegation entirely.
