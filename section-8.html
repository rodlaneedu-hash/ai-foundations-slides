Section 8

Prompting Essentials

Six Techniques for Effective Prompts

Each one closes the gap between vague output and useful output.
From Anthropic's prompt engineering guidance.

1
1

Provide Context

Don't just say what you want — say why, for whom, and in what situation.
Weak

"Tell me about climate change."

Stronger

"Explain three major impacts of climate change on agriculture in tropical regions, with examples from the past decade."

2
2

Show Examples of What "Good" Looks Like

An example does more than a paragraph of instructions.
Weak

"Convert this technical statement to plain language."

Stronger

"Here are two examples of how to convert technical jargon to plain language: [example 1] [example 2]. Now convert this statement using the same approach."

3
3

Specify Output Constraints

Length, format, structure, tone. Don't leave it to the AI's default.
Weak

"Design me a portfolio website."

Stronger

"Create a clean, single-page portfolio with these sections: About Me, Projects, Experience, Contact. Keep the language professional and concise."

4
4

Break Complex Tasks into Steps

Guide the AI's reasoning process. Ensure nothing gets skipped.
Weak

"Analyse this quarterly sales data."

Stronger

"Analyse this data by: 1. Identify top products, 2. Compare to last quarter, 3. Highlight unusual patterns, 4. Suggest possible reasons."

5

Ask It to Think First

Give it space to reason before responding.
Example prompt

"Before answering, think through this problem carefully. Consider the different factors involved, potential constraints, and various approaches before recommending the best solution."

6
6

Define the AI's Role, Style, or Tone

Tell it who it should be for this task. Shapes depth, framing, and voice.
Example prompt

"Explain how rainbows form from the perspective of an experienced science teacher speaking to a bright 10-year-old who's interested in science."

★

Secret Weapon

Ask the AI to help you write the prompt.

Try this

"I'm trying to get you to help me with [goal]. I'm not sure how to phrase my request to get the best results. Can you help me craft an effective prompt for this?"

The most powerful technique — and the one most people overlook.

8
Key Landing Point
When output is weak, diagnose which technique is missing.

Sharpen the context. Add an example. Constrain the output. Break it into steps. Ask it to think first. Define the role. Don't rewrite from scratch.
