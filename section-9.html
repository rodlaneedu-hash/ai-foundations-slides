Technique 1

Multi-Stage Prompting

Break the entire task into separate prompts. Review between each stage.

1 Scan
→
2 Extract
→
3 Compare
→
4 Draft
→
5 Critique

Each stage is a checkpoint. Verify before moving on.
Don't let the AI run the full pipeline unsupervised.

2
Technique 2

Iteration as a Habit

The best results come from the third or fourth exchange, not the first.

  • "That's too long. Cut it to half the length and keep only the strongest points."
  • "The tone is too formal. Rewrite as if explaining this to a colleague over coffee."
  • "You missed [specific thing]. Add it and restructure around it."
  • "That's good. Now challenge your own argument — what's the strongest counterpoint?"
3
Technique 3

Constraints and Guardrails

Tell the AI what not to do. Safety rails for high-stakes work.

  • "Do not fabricate sources or references."
  • "Highlight any uncertainties or assumptions you've made."
  • "Flag areas that require human review before this goes further."
  • "If you don't have enough information to answer confidently, say so."
  • "Do not include personal opinions or speculative claims."
Technique 4

The Diagnostic Habit

When output is weak, ask which letter is missing.

G
Goal & Role
What do you want? What role should the AI adopt? Sharpen the action verb.
C
Context
Who, why, what situation? Add the background so it can tailor its response.
S
Source
Point it to specific content. Don't let the AI guess when you have the material.
E
Expectation
What should the output look like? Specify format, tone, length, structure.
5
Technique 5

Ask the AI to Improve Your Approach

Turn the AI into a collaborator on the process, not just the content.

"I need to analyse these three policy documents. What would be the most effective sequence of prompts to do this thoroughly?"
"I've written this prompt but the output isn't what I need. What's wrong with my prompt and how would you improve it?"
"Suggest three ways I could break this task down into smaller steps."
9
Key Landing Point
Plan before you prompt.
Review before you proceed.

The common thread is intentionality. Always keep the human in the loop at every stage.
